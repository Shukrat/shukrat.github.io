Chasing and Fleeing


The Project

Built using Processing 2.2.1.

Chasing and Fleeing is a prototype coded in Processing 2, utilizing vectors to have cell-like objects chase and flee each other. In this program, all the cells must stay within the black border, avoid the grey obstacles, and chase or flee, depending on their color. The red cells chase the green ones to eat them. The green cells flee the red cells, and when caught turn into red cells themselves. Once all green cells have been eaten, the red cells wander around the scene.





My Responsibilities - Programmer

Utilized steering code to make cells move, turn, and track targets. Steering applies threats or targets from outside, and generates internal forces to move the cells in the desired direction. During testing, implemented debugging lines to visualize vectors and targets as necessary. Cells wander when not threatened or do not have a target.

Bonus: Programmed the background to simulate water particles moving behind the cells, with slow rotation.
